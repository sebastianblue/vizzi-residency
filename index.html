About the residency

Time, space, and good company.

The Alliance offers time and room to artists who want to think with their hands. Residents spend two weeks in shared studios, with space for sound, sculpture, print, and whatever a project asks for.

It's a self-directed experience. No classes, no critiques, nothing owed at the end. The point is the making and the company — to follow an idea without a deadline pulling at it.

Studios are shared with a small group, which is the whole idea: enough quiet to concentrate, enough overlap to be changed by someone working in another medium across the room.

The place

Hill country, in the interior.

The residency sits in the hills of inland Sicily — wheat, olives, and almonds; stone towns along the ridgelines; long golden light. It's about an hour from the city and a world away from the beaches.

Days are unhurried and meals are shared. Interior Sicily feeds you well and cheaply, and the table is where most of the real collaboration tends to happen.

At the heart of it is a family house — opened to artists, for the first time, as a place to gather and make.

What we provide

We take care of the space. You bring the rest.

Who is opening the doors

Built by artists, for artists.

The Vizzi family is opening the house as a working place for artists: a few residents at a time, a shared rhythm, and enough structure to make the time feel held without turning it into school.

The fine print

Details & how it works

Who should apply

Artists early in their careers — recent graduates and those a few years out — working in performance, music and composition, sculpture, printmaking, comics, photography, or visual art. The first cohorts are deliberately small and cross-disciplinary.

You should be comfortable working independently, and glad to share a studio and a table with people working in other media.

How to apply

The best first step is to register your interest — tell us who you are, what you make, and what you'd hope to do with two unhurried weeks.

Costs & financial support

The fee is $500 for two weeks, plus housing, which varies by cohort and room configuration. Artists cover their own travel, food, and materials. Material and travel support will be added as the residency can sustain it.

Dates & getting there

Sessions run in the gentle shoulder seasons — spring and fall — when the interior is at its best for working. Dates are announced ahead of each cohort.

The nearest airport is Palermo (Falcone–Borsellino), roughly 90 minutes away by road. Arrivals are batched on set days with a shared ride from the airport; there's also a rail option through the nearby valley for those travelling light.
